Technologies

Modern AI, Explained Simply

Understanding the tools that power today's intelligent business solutions.

🧠

Large Language Models (LLMs)

Big, general-purpose AI models trained on massive amounts of text, code, and images. Think of them as super advanced autocomplete — they predict what should come next based on patterns learned from huge datasets. GPT, Claude, Gemini, and open-source models are the engines behind modern AI.

✍️

Prompt Engineering

The skill of telling a model exactly what you want to get the result you're looking for. Defining format, audience, length, and goal in your instructions almost always produces better, more consistent outputs — whether you're chatting with AI or building a product.

🧩

Context Engineering

More than just a prompt — it's all the surrounding information that helps the model do its job: memory, instructions, documents, chat history, and user data. Better context means fewer hallucinations, more accurate answers, and less random behavior.

🔍

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

Lets AI look up your private data first, then answer based on that — not just its training data. Instead of guessing, it retrieves the right information and grounds its answer in your actual company documents, knowledge bases, and internal data.

🗺️

Embeddings & Vector Search

A way of turning text into numbers that actually mean something. Words with similar meanings end up near each other on a map. Vector databases store these embeddings and instantly find the closest matches — so searches work by meaning, not just exact keywords.

📏

Tokens & Context Windows

Tokens are how LLMs see text — they break words into smaller pieces. Context windows are like a whiteboard: the model can only see a certain amount at once. Newer models have huge context windows (even millions of tokens), but bigger windows cost more and can be slower.

🤖

AI Agents

Autonomous AI systems that don't just reply — they take action. An agent sets a goal, figures out the steps, calls tools and APIs, checks results, and loops until the job is done. This planning-and-looping capability is what makes them fundamentally different from simple chatbots.

🎛️

Fine-Tuning

Taking a base model and customizing it to do a specific job better. Rather than forcing behavior through prompts, you teach the model your style, domain, or output format. Useful when prompting alone feels unstable, too expensive, or inconsistent.

🔌

MCP (Model Context Protocol)

Like a USB port for your AI — a standard protocol that lets you plug in tools, APIs, databases, CRMs, calendars, and internal software. Before MCP, every integration was custom. Now, any model can connect to any service through a common interface.

🖼️

Multimodal AI

Models that can process images, text, video, and audio as inputs — and sometimes generate them as outputs. Look at a whiteboard photo and get an explanation, transcribe a voice memo, generate images from descriptions, or understand video. The real world isn't just text.

⚡

AI Workflows

Multi-stage automations with triggers, approvals, notifications, and integrations that chain together to handle entire business processes end-to-end.

📚

Knowledge Bases

Searchable company knowledge from documentation, SOPs, training manuals, and internal resources — accessible to AI and your team for instant, accurate answers.

🔗

API Integrations

Connect to OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, Groq, OpenRouter, Cohere, Mistral, Hugging Face, and more for maximum flexibility across AI providers.

🏠

Local AI

Deploy AI models on your own infrastructure using Ollama for complete data privacy, offline capability, and secure operations — your data never leaves your control.

🛡️

AI Security

Privacy-first governance, secure deployments, access controls, and compliance-ready AI implementations to protect your business data.
