Anatomy / 02

Three resources.
Three different clocks.

They are not interchangeable. Time disappears, money can often be rebuilt, and health determines how well everything else can be used.

Time / non-renewable

Hours are not enough.

Real time includes autonomy, attention, recovery, and unfragmented space—not just blank calendar cells.

  • High: control, presence, room for surprise
  • Low: urgency, fragmentation, borrowed sleep
  • Can be reclaimed with boundaries, proximity, systems, and money
Money / flexible

Options made visible.

Income, savings, assets, insurance, and resilience absorb shocks and create bargaining power.

  • High: needs covered, choices protected
  • Low: short-term costs dominate decisions
  • Can buy access and convenience—not meaning, presence, or guaranteed health
Health / capacity

The multiplier.

Physical and mental health, sleep, mobility, cognition, and energy shape how time and money can be used.

  • High: challenge is recoverable
  • Low: ordinary tasks cost more
  • Never a moral score; access, genetics, disability, environment, and luck matter
Default script / 03

The life-stage story.

A familiar pattern—not an age rule. Your responsibilities, access, health, and starting position may create a completely different path.

EARLY ADULTHOOD

Capable, curious, cash-light.

TIME ↑HEALTH ↑MONEY ↓

Learn, experiment, travel cheaply, build skills. The trap is treating health as infinite or selling every hour without building anything that compounds.

THE BUILDER YEARS

Assets rising, calendar shrinking.

MONEY ↑HEALTH ↔TIME ↓

Career, children, elder care, commuting, and administration converge. Buy back low-value time—but remember that presence, intimacy, sleep, and exercise resist outsourcing.

LATER LIFE

Freedom meets capacity.

TIME ↑MONEY ↑HEALTH ↓

Use money and time for access, support, adaptation, service, and meaning. Retirement does not guarantee wealth, freedom, health, social connection, or purpose.

Reality check: a young caregiver may have no time; a wealthy young person may have all three; a healthy retiree may have exceptional capacity. Disability, unemployment, inherited wealth, inequality, geography, and luck rewrite the script.

Patterns / 05

The combinations.

Two bright switches can produce very different lives. One bright switch may still feel like confinement. All three low is crisis, not an optimization challenge.

Time + HealthStudent, sabbatical, unemployment, or low-cost traveler. Freedom can coexist with financial insecurity.
Time + MoneyRetirement, disability, or recovery after burnout. Access improves; capacity may remain constrained.
Money + HealthExecutive, clinician, founder, dual-income parent. Capability is high, but life can become permanently deferred.
Time onlyTransition, isolation, forced unemployment, or recovery. Time without capacity or resources is not automatically freedom.
Money onlyHigh earnings under extreme demand. Wealth exists but is difficult to use or enjoy.
Health onlyPhysical potential without financial security or schedule control.
Field manual / 06

Convert. Combine. Protect.

Before accepting pure sacrifice, look for a conversion or synergy. Then install a floor so a temporary trade does not become permanent damage.

Conversions

Work more (Money up, Time down); take a pay cut (Time up, Money down); hire help (Money into Time); exercise and preventive care (Time and Money now for future Health); build skills (Time now for future Money and autonomy); sleep less (fake Time now, lower future capacity).

Synergies

Walk or cycle for transport; exercise with friends or family; batch-cook healthy food; live near work or support; learn a paid skill you enjoy; choose active travel. Synergy is not free—setup, coordination, and context determine whether it works.

Shocks

A new child, elder care, promotion, layoff, illness, inheritance, relocation, remote work, inflation, or burnout can change all three switches at once. Distinguish chosen ambition from imposed scarcity.

Ten-step review
  1. Define each switch personally.
  2. Audit hours, debt, savings, sleep, symptoms, and control.
  3. Name the season and its end date.
  4. Choose one resource to grow.
  5. Protect a non-negotiable floor.
  6. Name the cost in advance.
  7. Seek synergy first.
  8. Add guardrails.
  9. Review monthly or quarterly.
  10. Never let a temporary trade become an identity.
Evidence / 07

Metaphor, meet reality.

The 200-point limit is not science. These narrower research findings explain why the metaphor resonates.

55+ hrs

WHO/ILO found working at least 55 hours weekly associated with higher stroke and ischemic-heart-disease risk than 35–40 hours.

Time-rich

Time-affluence research links feeling that one has enough time with well-being, autonomy, relationships, and healthy activity.

Choice ≠ access

Fair pay, healthcare, childcare, accessibility, safe work, and social support shape whether “optimization” is possible at all.

Sources: WHO/ILO · Kasser & Sheldon · Whillans et al. · Giurge et al.
