Is Amplenote Worth Replacing Your Current Setup?

A breakdown of your situation and how Amplenote might fit in — weighing notes, tasks, and calendar against a Google-based workflow.

Current Setup

  • Google Docs: Notes and some tasks.
  • Google Tasks: Partial task management.
  • Google Calendar: Scheduling and some tasks (causing slowness).

Amplenote (Free Version)

  • Notes: Rich text formatting, organization with folders/tags.
  • Tasks: Integrated to-do lists within notes, drag-and-drop functionality.
  • Calendar: Basic calendar view (might not be a full replacement).

Possible Benefits of Amplenote

  • Organization: Amplenote could consolidate your notes and tasks into a single platform, potentially reducing clutter and improving workflow.
  • Task Management: Integrated tasks within notes might be more convenient than juggling Docs and Tasks.
  • Calendar: The basic calendar view could help visualize your schedule alongside notes and tasks (though not as powerful as Google Calendar).
  • Speed: By offloading tasks from Google Calendar, it might run faster.

Things to Consider

  • Learning Curve: Adapting to a new app might take some time.
  • Free Version Limitations: The free version of Amplenote might have limitations on storage or features compared to Google's free offerings.
  • Calendar Integration: Amplenote's calendar might not be as robust as Google Calendar, especially for complex scheduling needs.

Overall

Amplenote could be a good option if organization and task management within notes are priorities. However, if a powerful calendar is crucial, Google Calendar might be irreplaceable.

Recommendation: Try the free version of Amplenote and see if it fits your workflow. You can import notes from Docs and potentially reduce reliance on Google Calendar. If the calendar is essential, you might use both Amplenote and Google Calendar together.

2026 Note

Amplenote has matured since this comparison was first written. Its Task Score engine (which surfaces the right tasks based on urgency, importance, and effort), bi-directional note links, a daily Jots scratchpad, and a richer calendar all make the "notes + tasks + calendar in one place" pitch stronger than it was. The honest takeaway is unchanged: if a powerful standalone calendar is non-negotiable, keep Google Calendar in the loop — but for unifying notes and tasks, Amplenote is a solid single home.
